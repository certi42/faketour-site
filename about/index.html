About the Faketour

What is a Faketour?

The Faketour is a parody of Magic: the Gathering's Protour, where players create new Standard decks, and get to experience the new limited environment before the metagame has settled and the best decks and cards are well known. To this end, players must create their decks before any tournaments with the new format have been held, whcih means that they have the two week span from when all of the cards from the new set are revealed until the set's official release, when the first tournament will be held.

The Protour

Every three months, a new Magic: the Gathering expansion is released, and to celebrate this, Wizard of the Coast holds a tournament where pros come together from all across the world to battle it with the brand new cards.

Players compete in both draft and constructed, in an effort to crown the conclusive champion of the new set. The top performing players earn Pro Points, which gain them something, I would assume.

How does it work?

The Faketour consists of two portions, the first of which a Standard constructed tournament, where players build cards incorporating cards from the newest set. The form of the other half can vary, but previous Faketours have included:

  • Draft
  • Sealed
  • Frontier
  • Modern
  • Cube

    The most frequently included format is draft, but whatever the choice, the purpose is always to highlight the impact of the newly released set.

    Players play a three-round swiss tournament for each format, resulting in six rounds total. The best four players collectively then play a single-elimination Standard tournament to crown the champion. Rounds in the swiss portion are best-of-three, while games in the single single-elimination portion are best-of-five.
